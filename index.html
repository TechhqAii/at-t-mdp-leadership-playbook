D

Questions that travel well

  • “What do your strongest leaders do before they have the title?”
  • “Where do you see the biggest friction between strategy and field execution?”
  • “What should someone at my level learn to see earlier?”
E

Graceful exits

  • “I don’t want to monopolize you—thank you. I’m taking that point with me.”
  • “That was useful. I’m going to let you keep circulating.”
  • “I’d like to follow up with the action I take from this, if that’s okay.”
F

Do not

  • Lead with every award.
  • Pitch your app before there is a doorway.
  • Ask for a promotion, job, or “pick your brain.”
  • Gossip about succession or internal politics.
  • Talk longer than the senior leader.

Body language: phone away · hands visible · pace 10% slower · finish sentences · hold eye contact · leave space

Success: five real conversations · three follow-ups · one continuing relationship
